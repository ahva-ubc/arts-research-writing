Workshop Description

Join Rémi Castonguay, VRC Curator; Sara Ellis, Art & Visual Literacy Librarian; and Jessica Law, Graduate Academic Assistant, for an exploration of visual arts resources tools. We’ll spend a good deal of time on basic research and writing resources that can help you  guide your students through that process. If you are faculty or a TA helping students write papers, this might be a great session to attend. If you are a new student looking to learn more about the rich array of UBC visual arts resources, this is also a great session to attend!

Library

General

  • The best place to get a thorough orientation to Library research is the Get Research Help portal: https://help.library.ubc.ca/
  • Once a student is ready to begin searching, a good starting place is Summon, the single search bar / search engine located right on the Library homepage
    https://library.ubc.ca 

  • Take advantage of the embedded tools to facilitate keyword searching and link out to related content
  • E.g., “More Info” link in Summon (see screenshot below) →
    “Subjects” organize books using Library of Congress Classification and are useful for expanding keyword searches and linking to related material

  • From there, a search can be expanded using the Browse the Catalogue feature
  • e.g., Subject begins with or Subject contains options (see screenshots below)
  • Students are often not aware of this tool, which breaks topics down into specific sub-sections or categories  


  • Lean Library browser extension:
    https://guides.library.ubc.ca/connect/ll
  • After downloading, the extension will notify you if you’re on a website that contains content the Library has subscription access to. Then, it’s just a single click to open the site through the Library’s systems and get access to all that licensed material.
